Understanding the Model Statistics

This page explains the advanced basketball statistics used in our March Madness prediction model. Each feature was selected because it helps measure team efficiency, strength, and play style.

Seed

A team's tournament ranking (1-16). Lower seeds (like 1 or 2) are typically stronger teams based on overall season performance.

AdjEM (Adjusted Efficiency Margin)

A measure of how many points per 100 possessions a team outscores its opponents by, adjusted for strength of schedule. Higher values indicate stronger teams.

AdjTempo

The estimated number of possessions per game, adjusted for opponent strength. Higher tempo teams play faster-paced basketball.

FG2Pct (2-Point Field Goal Percentage)

The percentage of two-point shots a team makes. This measures scoring efficiency inside the arc.

ORPct (Offensive Rebound Percentage)

The percentage of available offensive rebounds a team grabs. Higher values indicate strong second-chance scoring ability.

TOPct (Turnover Percentage)

The percentage of possessions that end in a turnover. Lower values are better, as they indicate better ball control.
